**Units: all columns are bare wall time in ns/call — smaller is faster.** A `parallel/openblas` wall-time ratio **< 1.0** means parallel is faster; the firm bar is par ≤ ob in every cell. (The separate interleaved per-routine harness reports the same par/ob wall-time ratio, where < 1.0 = par faster — the same direction. See `docs/optimization-findings.md` → "Reporting convention".)

> ⚠️ **Methodology + how to read the OMP=4 gaps.** This table is a **min-of-N interleaved** sweep — several full passes over the whole surface (`REPS` runs), with `aggregate.py` keeping the per-cell minimum so transient drift/contention cancels — NOT a single-shot screen. Serial (`par1/ob1`) numbers are therefore trustworthy at the cell level, and near-parity cells (**0.95–1.05 band**) are at parity. **The remaining large `par4/ob4` ratios are STRUCTURAL by design, not regressions:** the OpenBLAS-style reference threads *every* shape unconditionally, while the parallel overlay threads selectively. Confirmed from `par4/par1` (≈1.00 ⇒ par ran serial; ≈0.25 ⇒ par threaded 4×): (1) **L1 RMW** (`*axpy` `*scal` `*copy` `*swap` `*rot*`, all families) — par is intentionally serial in the bandwidth-bound regime (write-bound RMW doesn't benefit; par's serial ties/beats ob serial), and threads only where cache-resident bandwidth scales. (2) **band tri-solve** (`*tbsv`, all families) — O(N·K) work with a K-deep loop-carried recurrence; not parallelizable, and OpenBLAS doesn't thread it either. (3) **scalar / x87-floor soft spots** — tiny-absolute-ns scalars (`*rotmg`, `*rotm`) and disassembled-clean x87 outer-loop scheduling floors. None is a correctness or serial-speed regression; against the migrated Fortran baseline (`migrated-serial`, what ships) par is at-or-ahead everywhere. The per-family threading-coverage frontier and its closing commits are tracked in `docs/archive/performance-gap-20260607.md`. Fine-grained near-parity verdicts on the rewritten L3 set (`esymm` `esyrk` `etrsm` `etrmm` `ysymm` `yhemm` `yherk` `yher2k`, and their q/x/m/w twins) come from the **per-routine interleaved harness** (`reports/cmp5/*_interleaved.md`).
